Common English Mistakes Haryanvi Speakers Make

Improving your fluency starts with fixing these small but common errors tailored for our local accent.

The "MTI" Challenge

Mother Tongue Influence (MTI) is natural. In Haryana, our dialect is strong and energetic, but sometimes this energy translates into English errors. At Unnat Tuition Center, we help hundreds of students in Kurukshetra polish their language skills.

1. The "IS" and "ARE" Confusion

Mistake: "He are going to market." / "They is playing."

Correction: "He is going." / "They are playing."

Tip: Remember: Singular (One person) = Is. Plural (Many people) = Are.

2. Pronunciation: 'S' vs 'Sh'

Many students confuse 'Sea' and 'She'. This is very common in our region.
Practice this tongue twister: "She sells sea shells on the sea shore."

3. Overusing "Only"

Mistake: "I am coming tomorrow only." (Direct translation of "kal hi aaunga")

Correction: "I will come tomorrow." OR "I am just coming tomorrow."

4. Removing Fillers like "Na"

Mistake: "You are coming, na?"

Correction: "Are you coming?"

In professional English, we avoid ending sentences with "na". It makes you sound less confident in interviews.
